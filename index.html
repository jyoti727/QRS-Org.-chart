HR Duties and Responsibilities

  • Developing HR Strategies: Create and implement HR strategies that align with the overall business objectives.
  • Recruitment and Selection: Manage the recruitment process, including job postings, reviewing applications, conducting interviews, and selecting candidates.
  • Onboarding New Employees: Facilitate the onboarding process for new hires, ensuring they are integrated smoothly into the organization.
  • Employee Relations: Act as a bridge between management and employees by addressing grievances, resolving conflicts, and fostering a positive work environment.
  • Performance Management: Oversee performance appraisal systems to ensure high performance across the organization.
  • Training and Development: Assess training needs and develop training programs to enhance employee skills and career growth.
  • Compensation and Benefits Management: Maintain pay plans, benefits programs, and ensure competitive compensation structures to attract and retain talent.
  • Compliance with Labor Laws: Ensure that all HR practices comply with labor laws and regulations to mitigate legal risks.
  • Employee Records Management: Maintain accurate employee records related to attendance, medical leave, and other HR documentation as mandated by law.
  • Policy Development and Implementation: Develop, implement, and update HR policies regarding working conditions, equal opportunities, disciplinary procedures, etc.
  • Organizational Culture Promotion: Work with management to cultivate an organizational culture that supports strategic goals and promotes diversity and inclusion.
  • Payroll Processing: Ensure timely and accurate payroll processing, including managing deductions for taxes and benefits.
  • Conducting Disciplinary Actions: Handle disciplinary actions appropriately to maintain workplace standards while supporting employee development.
  • Reporting and Decision Support: Provide management with HR metrics for informed decision-making regarding workforce management.
  • Employee Support Programs: Develop programs that support employee well-being, including mental health resources and career development paths.
  • Negotiation with External Agencies: Liaise with external agencies for labor relations issues or benefits negotiations.
  • Crisis Management: Address workplace crises effectively while maintaining employee trust and morale.
  • Continuous Improvement Initiatives: Identify areas for improvement within HR processes to enhance efficiency and effectiveness in meeting organizational needs.
